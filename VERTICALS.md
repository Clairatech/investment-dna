# Vertical Adaptation Guide

The core Investment DNA framework is strategy-agnostic — 9 context files, a scorecard, and an interview protocol. But the *content* of those files varies meaningfully across private market asset classes. This guide covers how to adapt each template for three verticals.

---

## Private Equity

**What changes:** The default framework fits PE most naturally. Key additions are around value creation specifics, deal sourcing, and platform/bolt-on structures.

### Firm Identity

- **Fund lifecycle:** Emphasize vintage year, deployment pace, and remaining investable capital. PE funds have defined investment periods (typically 3-5 years) followed by harvest periods.
- **Target ownership:** Majority (buyout) vs. significant minority (growth). This shapes everything downstream.
- **Fund economics:** Standard 2/20 with variations. Note hurdle rate, catch-up, and GP commit — LPs scrutinize these.

### Investment DNA

- **Return driver model:** Decompose explicitly into: (1) revenue growth, (2) margin improvement, (3) multiple expansion, (4) leverage/financial engineering, (5) add-on M&A. Be honest about which drives returns — many PE firms claim "operational value creation" but returns come from leverage and multiple expansion.
- **Value creation approach:** Specify whether you are hands-on operators, board-level governance, talent upgraders, capital allocators, or a combination. Detail the 100-day plan approach.
- **Add: buy-and-build strategy.** If you run platform + bolt-on playbooks, describe the acquisition integration model, target add-on profiles, and how many bolt-ons per platform is typical.

### Investment Criteria

- **Financial thresholds:** EBITDA floor (not just revenue), margin profile, capex intensity, working capital dynamics, cash conversion.
- **Valuation:** Entry EV/EBITDA range by sector, maximum leverage at entry (debt/EBITDA), equity cushion requirements.
- **Management:** PE-specific: willingness of founder to roll equity, openness to professionalization, track record under PE ownership if a secondary buyout.
- **Add: deal sourcing channel.** Track proprietary vs. intermediated vs. auction. Win rates and pricing differ dramatically.

### Risk Framework

- **Leverage risk:** Debt covenants, refinancing risk, interest rate sensitivity, debt maturity profile.
- **J-curve management:** Early losses from fees and slow deployment. LP expectations for DPI milestones.
- **Concentration:** PE portfolios are inherently concentrated (10-15 positions). Position sizing discipline is critical.
- **Exit planning:** Start at entry. Map the 2-3 likely exit paths (strategic sale, sponsor-to-sponsor, IPO) and required conditions for each.

### Portfolio Tracking

- **Add fields:** Platform vs. standalone, number of bolt-on acquisitions, total invested (including bolt-ons), current leverage (net debt/EBITDA), LTM EBITDA vs. entry EBITDA, management team changes since entry.
- **Valuation approach:** Mark-to-model (comparable multiples, DCF, recent transaction) rather than market price. Note the methodology.

### Scorecard Adjustments

| Dimension | Weight | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| DNA Fit | 20% | Default weight works |
| Criteria Match | 15% | |
| Value Creation Potential | 15% | Specific operational levers identified |
| Team Quality | 15% | Will this team execute the plan? |
| Thesis Alignment | 10% | |
| Market / Exit Visibility | 10% | Can you see the exit from here? |
| Portfolio Fit | 10% | Concentration, sector, vintage |
| Risk/Return | 5% | |

## Private Credit

**What changes:** Fundamentally different return model. You are lending, not owning — downside protection and yield matter more than upside capture.

### Firm Identity

- **Fund structure:** Specify: direct lending fund, BDC, CLO, mezzanine fund, opportunistic/distressed, separately managed account. Each has different return targets and constraints.
- **Mandate:** Senior secured, unitranche, second lien, mezzanine, preferred equity, or opportunistic across the capital structure. State where you play and where you don't.
- **Target returns:** Express as yield (spread over SOFR), total return (including fees), and loss-adjusted return. Target loss rates and recovery assumptions. Typical ranges: senior direct lending 8-12%, unitranche 10-14%, mezz 14-18%.
- **Check size:** Hold size vs. underwrite size. Syndication and sell-down strategy.
- **Fund economics:** Management fee on deployed vs. committed capital (matters enormously). Incentive fee structure (income-based vs. total return). Hurdle rate.

### Investment DNA

- **Return driver model:** Replace the equity-oriented model entirely:
  - **Yield:** Base rate + credit spread. Current vs. expected spread tightening/widening.
  - **Fees:** OID (original issue discount), commitment fees, prepayment penalties, call protection, amendment fees.
  - **Losses avoided:** Gross-to-net spread. Target loss rate and recovery rate.
  - **What you do NOT rely on:** Equity upside, warrant kickers (unless mezzanine), multiple expansion.
- **Core beliefs:** What do you believe about credit cycles, sponsor behavior, documentation trends, or recovery rates that others get wrong?
- **Edge:** Sourcing (sponsor relationships, direct origination), structuring (documentation, covenant negotiation), workout/restructuring capability, speed of execution, hold capacity.
- **What you avoid:** Over-levered sponsor deals with aggressive add-backs, covenant-lite documentation without adequate protection, cyclical businesses without asset coverage, "story credits" where the thesis requires everything to go right.

### Investment Criteria

Replace equity-oriented criteria with credit-specific filters:

- **Leverage:** Maximum total leverage (debt/EBITDA), maximum senior leverage, minimum interest coverage ratio (EBITDA/cash interest), minimum fixed charge coverage.
- **EBITDA quality:** Maximum EBITDA add-back as % of reported EBITDA, minimum historical EBITDA track record, revenue concentration limits.
- **Business quality:** Minimum revenue scale, recurring/contractual revenue preference, gross margin floor, capex intensity ceiling, working capital volatility tolerance.
- **Sponsor quality:** Approved sponsor list, minimum fund size, track record requirements, equity cushion requirements (loan-to-value).
- **Documentation:** Covenant requirements (leverage, fixed charge, capex, restricted payments), definition scrutiny (EBITDA definitions, permitted baskets, builder baskets), call protection minimums.
- **Structure:** Maximum loan-to-value, collateral requirements, guarantor coverage, lien priority.
- **Automatic passes:** Sponsor leverage above Xth turn, EBITDA below $Y, no financial maintenance covenants, single-customer concentration above Z%.

### Risk Framework

- **Portfolio construction:** Number of positions (typically 30-80+ for diversification), max single-name exposure, sector concentration limits, sponsor concentration limits.
- **Credit risk metrics:** Weighted average leverage, weighted average interest coverage, weighted average LTV, portfolio yield, non-accrual rate.
- **Rate sensitivity:** Floating vs. fixed rate mix, floor levels, impact of rate moves on borrower coverage ratios.
- **Loss modeling:** Expected loss rate, stress loss scenarios, recovery rate assumptions by collateral type and seniority.
- **Concentration risk:** By sponsor, industry, geography, loan size, maturity.
- **Watch list criteria:** Define triggers (missed projections by X%, leverage above Y, coverage below Z, covenant waiver requests).
- **Workout/restructuring:** In-house capability vs. outsourced, experience with amendments, extensions, DIP financing, credit bidding.

### Portfolio Tracking

Replace equity portfolio fields with:

- **Per position:** Borrower name, sponsor, industry, facility type (revolver/TL/delayed draw), commitment amount, funded amount, interest rate (spread + floor), maturity date, current leverage (debt/EBITDA), current coverage (EBITDA/interest), LTV, covenant levels and current cushion, internal risk rating, accrual status.
- **Portfolio level:** Weighted average spread, weighted average leverage, weighted average LTV, % floating rate, maturity schedule, sector breakdown, sponsor breakdown, non-accrual rate, unrealized markdown as % of portfolio.

### Scorecard Adjustments

| Dimension | Weight | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Downside Protection | 25% | Collateral, coverage, LTV, covenant cushion |
| Business / Credit Quality | 20% | Cash flow stability, margin, customer concentration |
| Documentation Quality | 15% | Covenants, definitions, baskets, protections |
| Sponsor Quality | 15% | Track record, equity cushion, willingness to support |
| Yield / Structure | 10% | Adequate compensation for risk taken |
| Portfolio Fit | 10% | Concentration, maturity, sector balance |
| DNA Fit | 5% | Alignment with stated credit strategy |

## Commercial Real Estate (CRE)

**What changes:** Asset-backed investing with physical properties. Returns depend on location, property type, lease structure, and capital markets conditions. Both equity and debt strategies exist.

### Firm Identity

- **Fund structure:** Specify: core, core-plus, value-add, opportunistic (for equity); senior, mezzanine, bridge, construction (for debt). Risk/return profile varies enormously across these categories.
- **Mandate:** Property types (multifamily, office, industrial, retail, hospitality, life sciences, data centers, self-storage). Geographic focus at MSA level, not just country.
- **Target returns:** Equity — express as net IRR and equity multiple. Distinguish between current yield and total return. Typical ranges: core 6-8%, value-add 12-18%, opportunistic 18%+. Debt — express as yield, LTV, and loss-adjusted return.
- **Check size:** Per-property equity check and total capitalization (equity + debt). Maximum single-asset exposure.
- **LP base:** CRE allocations often come from separate buckets within institutional portfolios. Note whether your LPs have CRE-specific allocation targets.

### Investment DNA

- **Return driver model:** Decompose by source:
  - **Income return:** NOI yield (cap rate) at entry, contractual rent escalations, lease-up of vacancy.
  - **Value creation:** Renovations/repositioning, re-tenanting at higher rents, operational efficiency, entitlements/rezoning.
  - **Capital markets:** Cap rate compression, refinancing at lower rates, market cycle timing.
  - **Development yield:** For ground-up or major renovation — cost basis vs. stabilized value (development spread/yield-on-cost).
- **Core beliefs:** Views on urbanization/suburbanization, remote work impact, supply cycles, interest rate sensitivity, property type secular trends (e.g., industrial tailwinds from e-commerce, office headwinds).
- **Edge:** Local market knowledge, operating platform, tenant relationships, entitlement/zoning expertise, construction management, distressed/off-market sourcing capability.
- **What you avoid:** Commodity assets in oversupplied markets, single-tenant risk without credit backing, development without pre-leasing, negative leverage (cap rate below debt cost) without clear path to positive leverage.

### Investment Criteria

- **Property-level filters:**
  - Property type(s) and sub-types (e.g., Class A suburban multifamily, last-mile industrial, grocery-anchored retail)
  - Target MSAs / submarkets and exclusions
  - Minimum / maximum property size (units, SF, keys)
  - Vintage / condition (year built, recent renovation)
  - Occupancy floor at acquisition vs. stabilized target
  - Environmental / structural deal-breakers (flood zone, brownfield, seismic risk)
- **Financial thresholds:**
  - Entry cap rate range by property type
  - Maximum price per unit / per SF
  - Minimum cash-on-cash yield (for income strategies)
  - Minimum unlevered IRR
  - Maximum basis relative to replacement cost
  - Rent growth assumptions (in-place vs. market, mark-to-market opportunity)
- **Tenancy / lease:**
  - Weighted average lease term (WALT) requirements
  - Credit quality of tenant base
  - Tenant concentration limits (max single tenant % of revenue)
  - Lease structure preferences (NNN, gross, modified gross)
  - Rent roll rollover exposure by year
- **Capital structure:**
  - Target LTV at acquisition
  - Debt type preference (agency, CMBS, bank, life company, bridge)
  - Fixed vs. floating rate preference
  - Minimum DSCR (debt service coverage ratio)
  - Recourse vs. non-recourse requirements
- **For CRE debt strategies:**
  - Maximum LTV / LTC (loan-to-cost)
  - Minimum DSCR / debt yield
  - Sponsor net worth and liquidity requirements
  - Collateral requirements and recourse carve-outs
  - Construction completion guarantees (for construction lending)

### Risk Framework

- **Portfolio construction:** Number of assets, max single-asset exposure, property type diversification, geographic diversification, vintage diversification, tenant diversification.
- **Market risk:** Interest rate sensitivity (both cap rates and debt cost), supply pipeline monitoring by MSA/submarket, rent growth sustainability, occupancy cycle position.
- **Asset-level risk:** Tenant rollover schedule, capital expenditure requirements, deferred maintenance, environmental liability, entitlement risk.
- **Leverage risk:** LTV creep from value declines, refinancing risk (maturity walls), floating rate exposure, covenant triggers (DSCR, LTV).
- **Liquidity risk:** CRE is illiquid with long transaction timelines. Disposition assumptions under stress.
- **Stress scenarios:** Rising rates + cap rate expansion, recession + tenant defaults, oversupply in target markets, remote work structural shift (office).
- **Exit planning:** Hold-to-stabilize vs. hold-to-maturity vs. opportunistic. Map likely buyers (institutional, 1031 exchange, REIT, foreign capital).

### Portfolio Tracking

- **Per position:** Property name, address, MSA, property type, acquisition date, acquisition price, equity invested, total capitalization, current NOI (actual vs. underwritten), occupancy (physical and economic), in-place cap rate, current estimated value, hold period to date, levered/unlevered IRR (actual vs. underwritten), debt terms (rate, maturity, LTV), major tenants and lease expirations, capital improvement status, risk rating.
- **Portfolio level:** Weighted average cap rate, weighted average occupancy, property type breakdown, geographic breakdown, total leverage (portfolio LTV), rent roll expiration schedule, capital improvement budget vs. spent, NOI growth (same-store).

### Scorecard Adjustments

| Dimension | Weight | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Location / Market | 20% | Submarket fundamentals, supply/demand, rent growth |
| Asset Quality | 15% | Physical condition, design, functionality, tenancy |
| DNA Fit | 15% | Alignment with strategy (core vs. value-add vs. opportunistic) |
| Cash Flow / Income | 15% | NOI stability, lease term, credit quality of tenants |
| Value Creation Potential | 10% | Repositioning, lease-up, renovation, entitlement upside |
| Capital Structure | 10% | Leverage, debt terms, refinancing risk |
| Portfolio Fit | 10% | Property type / geography / vintage diversification |
| Exit Visibility | 5% | Buyer universe, market liquidity, hold period clarity |

## Adapting the Interview Protocol

The 9-session structure works across all verticals — the *questions* change. When running an interview:

- **Sessions 1-2 (Identity, DNA):** Swap in the return driver model and mandate language for your vertical. A credit manager's "where do returns come from?" answer is structurally different from a PE firm's.
- **Session 3 (Criteria):** Use the vertical-specific criteria sections above rather than the default equity-oriented questions.
- **Session 5 (Risk):** Emphasize the risk factors most relevant to your vertical — leverage and covenants for credit, rate sensitivity and occupancy for CRE, factor exposure for public markets.
- **Session 6 (Portfolio):** Use the vertical-specific portfolio tracking fields. A CRE portfolio review covers NOI, occupancy, and lease expirations, not ARR and burn rate.
- **Sessions 4, 7-9** work largely as-is across verticals.

## Adapting the Scorecard

The default scorecard weights are designed for equity-oriented PE/VC. Each vertical section above includes a recommended scorecard variant. When customizing:

1. Copy `evaluation/deal-scorecard.md`
2. Replace dimensions and weights with the vertical-specific version
3. Update the scoring guide (1-5) to reflect vertical-specific definitions of "Exceptional" vs. "Poor"
4. Save alongside the original — you may evaluate cross-asset-class opportunities
