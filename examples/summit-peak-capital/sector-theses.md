# Sector Theses

## Active Theses

### Vertical SaaS in Healthcare Operations

- **Sector/theme:** Healthcare IT — back-office and mid-office operations
- **Conviction level:** High
- **Core thesis:** US healthcare spends $250B+ annually on administrative overhead. Regulation (HIPAA, interoperability mandates) creates high switching costs and barriers to entry. Vertical SaaS platforms that embed deeply into clinical and billing workflows become systems of record that are nearly impossible to rip out.
- **What we're looking for:** Companies selling to provider groups (20-500 physicians), ambulatory surgery centers, or specialty clinics. Revenue model should be SaaS (not per-transaction). Must handle PHI compliantly.
- **Key risks to thesis:** CMS reimbursement changes that reduce provider margins; Epic/Cerner expanding into mid-market and squeezing point solutions.
- **Time horizon for thesis:** 5+ years — regulatory tailwinds (interoperability rules, price transparency) are multi-year catalysts
- **Current exposure:** MedFlow Systems (Fund I), ClinicOS (Fund II)

### AI-Native Developer Tools

- **Sector/theme:** Software development infrastructure — AI-augmented coding, testing, and deployment
- **Conviction level:** High
- **Core thesis:** Every software team will adopt AI-assisted development within 3 years. The winners won't be the foundation model providers but the workflow tools that integrate AI into existing developer habits. PLG adoption is natural in this market — developers try tools individually before teams adopt.
- **What we're looking for:** Companies with bottom-up developer adoption (>1,000 active users), open-source community engagement, and a clear enterprise upsell motion. Must be model-agnostic (not locked to one LLM provider).
- **Key risks to thesis:** GitHub Copilot / Microsoft bundling kills standalone tools; open-source alternatives commoditize the space; AI capability improvements make simpler tools sufficient.
- **Time horizon for thesis:** 3–5 years — market is forming now; winners will be clear by 2027
- **Current exposure:** CodeLattice (Fund II)

### Compliance Infrastructure for Financial Services

- **Sector/theme:** Fintech infrastructure — regulatory compliance, KYC/AML, reporting
- **Conviction level:** Medium-High
- **Core thesis:** Post-SVB, post-FTX regulatory scrutiny on fintechs and banks is intensifying. Compliance spend is growing 15-20% annually. Companies that automate compliance workflows (not just flag transactions) become embedded in operational processes with 95%+ retention.
- **What we're looking for:** B2B SaaS selling to banks, credit unions, or fintechs. Must replace manual processes (not just layer on top). Strong data moat from processing real regulatory data.
- **Key risks to thesis:** Regulatory pendulum swings back to lighter touch; large incumbents (FIS, Fiserv) acquire or replicate.
- **Time horizon for thesis:** 4–6 years
- **Current exposure:** None yet — actively sourcing

## Thesis Evolution

- Increased conviction on healthcare vertical SaaS after seeing MedFlow's NDR hit 145% in Fund I. The stickiness thesis is playing out faster than expected.
- Reduced conviction on horizontal B2B payments — Stripe/Adyen are commoditizing the space faster than we anticipated. Pivoted focus to compliance infrastructure instead.
- Added AI-native developer tools thesis in Q3 2024 after seeing CodeLattice's organic growth metrics. Developer PLG is the strongest bottoms-up adoption we've seen since Slack.
