# Risk Framework

## Portfolio Construction

- **Target number of positions:** 12–15 per fund
- **Max position size (at cost):** 12% of fund
- **Max position size (at market):** 25% of fund (will trim if materially exceeded)
- **Max sector concentration:** 35% in any single sector thesis
- **Max geography concentration:** N/A (mandate is North America only)
- **Vintage year diversification:** Deploy over 3–4 years; no more than 40% of fund in any single year
- **Reserve ratio:** 40% of fund reserved for follow-ons

## Risk Tolerances

- **Loss tolerance per position:** Accept total loss on up to 2 positions per fund; no single loss should represent >12% of fund at cost
- **Max portfolio drawdown tolerance:** 25% unrealized drawdown before triggering full portfolio review with IC
- **Liquidity requirements:** Minimal — closed-end fund structure means no redemption pressure. Cash management for management fees and expenses only.
- **Currency risk approach:** N/A — USD-denominated investments only
- **Interest rate sensitivity:** Moderate — rising rates compress growth multiples but don't affect underlying business quality. We model exits at rate-adjusted multiples.

## Risk Factors Monitored

- **Concentration risk:** Quarterly review of top 3 position weights; alert if any position >20% of fund NAV
- **Key person risk:** Flag any portfolio company where CEO departure would fundamentally break the thesis
- **Customer concentration:** Monitor quarterly; flag if any portfolio company's top customer exceeds 25% of revenue
- **Regulatory/political risk:** Annual regulatory landscape review for healthcare and fintech portfolio companies
- **Technology/obsolescence risk:** Semi-annual review of competitive landscape; specific focus on AI disruption to existing portfolio
- **Macro sensitivity:** Stress test portfolio quarterly against rate hike, recession, and credit tightening scenarios
- **Correlation between positions:** Track sector and customer overlap; concerned about correlated drawdowns in healthcare if CMS cuts reimbursement

## Hedging & Mitigation

- No financial hedging instruments — manage risk through portfolio construction and position sizing
- Diversification across 3+ sector theses, with no thesis >35% of fund
- Operating partner engagement as active risk mitigation — if we see early warning signs, we increase involvement before problems compound

## Stress Scenarios

| Scenario | Portfolio Impact | Mitigation |
|----------|-----------------|------------|
| 2022-style rate shock (rates +200bps) | Exit multiples compress 20-30%; IRR drops ~5 points | Entry price discipline; model exits at compressed multiples; extend hold periods |
| Healthcare reimbursement cut (10% CMS reduction) | MedFlow and ClinicOS revenue growth slows; 15-20% NAV impact | <35% healthcare concentration; diversification within healthcare sub-segments |
| AI disruption to dev tools (Copilot bundles everything) | CodeLattice faces existential competitive pressure | Invested because of workflow depth, not AI capability alone; monitor monthly |
| Broad SaaS recession (enterprise budgets cut 15%) | Portfolio-wide growth deceleration; 2-3 companies may need bridge rounds | Reserve ratio at 40%; selective follow-on only for category leaders |

## Position Sizing Philosophy

- Base check: $15–20M for standard conviction deals
- Upsize to $25–35M for highest-conviction deals (strong PLG metrics, proven management team, clear category leadership)
- Never invest more than 12% of fund in initial check regardless of conviction — humility about our forecasting ability
- Follow-on reserved for companies hitting or exceeding plan; do not throw good money after bad

## Exit Triggers

- **Thesis invalidation signals:** NDR drops below 110% for two consecutive quarters; key product thesis disproven; management team turnover at C-suite level without adequate replacement
- **Valuation-driven exit:** If a position reaches >5x MOIC and a credible exit path exists, begin active exit process
- **Opportunity cost threshold:** If a position is performing at <1.5x MOIC after 3 years with no clear inflection ahead, consider secondary sale
- **Time-based review triggers:** Formal exit discussion at IC for every position at year 4; mandatory exit plan by year 6
