# Market Views

**Last updated:** 2025-03-15

## Macro Outlook

- **Economic cycle view:** Mid-cycle expansion with elevated uncertainty. Soft landing appears to have worked, but growth is moderating.
- **GDP growth expectation:** 2.0–2.5% US real GDP for 2025
- **Inflation view:** Sticky at 2.5–3.0%; services inflation persistent. Not a portfolio killer but constrains rate cuts.
- **Interest rate trajectory:** Fed holds at 4.25–4.50% through H1 2025; 1-2 cuts in H2 if inflation cooperates. No return to ZIRP.
- **Credit conditions:** Tightening for lower-quality borrowers; well-capitalized SaaS companies with recurring revenue can still raise.
- **Liquidity environment:** Dry powder in growth equity is high (~$300B+); competition for quality deals remains intense.

## Market Conditions

- **Public market valuations:** Fair to slightly rich for high-quality SaaS (10-15x NTM revenue for >30% growers). Not 2021 levels but not cheap.
- **Private market valuations:** Corrected from 2021 peaks but still elevated for top-quartile companies. 15-25x ARR for strong growth-stage SaaS is the new normal.
- **Fundraising environment:** LP appetite for growth equity recovering after 2022-2023 pullback. DPI pressure on GPs is real — LPs want distributions.
- **Exit environment:** IPO window cracking open — several SaaS IPOs in pipeline for 2025. M&A active with strategics acquiring at 8-12x revenue for profitable companies. Secondary market liquid for high-quality positions.
- **Deal flow quality:** Improving. 2020-2021 vintage companies that survived the correction are battle-tested. Seeing more capital-efficient businesses than in the ZIRP era.
- **Competition for deals:** Intense for top-decile opportunities. Crossover hedge funds less active than 2021 but Tiger/Coatue still competing. Differentiation through value-add is critical.

## Key Assumptions

1. Interest rates remain above 3.5% for the foreseeable future — no return to 0% rates that inflated 2021 multiples
2. AI adoption accelerates enterprise software spend (net positive for our portfolio) rather than cannibalizes it
3. IPO market reopens meaningfully in 2025-2026, providing exit path for Fund I positions

## What Has Changed

- IPO market warmer than 6 months ago — Klaviyo, Rubrik, and Reddit IPOs created a template. MedFlow is tracking toward IPO-readiness.
- AI disruption concern has evolved: initial fear of SaaS replacement is giving way to AI-as-accelerant narrative. Our portfolio companies are integrating AI features (net positive for retention and pricing power).
- DPI pressure from LPs is real and increasing. Need to show distributions from Fund I within 18 months. This influences exit timing.

## Implications for Deployment

- **Deployment pace:** Maintain — deploy 60% of Fund II by end of 2025
- **Selectivity adjustment:** Slightly more selective on entry price; emphasize profitability path over pure growth
- **Sector tilts:** Overweight compliance/fintech infrastructure (regulatory tailwind); maintain healthcare; cautious on crowded AI-native categories
- **Structure preferences:** Increasing use of structured equity with downside protection (participating preferred or ratchets) in deals where we're paying >20x ARR
