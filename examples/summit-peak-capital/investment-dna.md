# Investment DNA

## Core Beliefs

1. **Product-led growth compounds faster and cheaper than sales-led growth.** Companies where the product is the primary acquisition channel build deeper moats and more capital-efficient businesses.
2. **Net dollar retention is the single best predictor of long-term SaaS value.** NDR >130% means the product is expanding inside customers without proportional sales spend.
3. **The best growth equity returns come from funding companies that have already found product-market fit but haven't yet built the go-to-market machine.** This is the inflection point where capital efficiency is highest.
4. **Management teams that have operated at 2x their current scale make the scaling journey dramatically less risky.** First-time scaling is where most breakdowns happen.
5. **Market timing matters less than entry price discipline.** Overpaying at the Series B destroys returns regardless of market conditions.
6. **Vertical SaaS in regulated industries creates durable moats that horizontal competitors can't easily replicate.**

## Return Driver Model

- **Primary return driver:** Revenue growth (2.5–4x revenue growth over hold period)
- **Secondary drivers:** Margin expansion as operating leverage kicks in; moderate multiple expansion for companies that graduate from growth equity to IPO-ready
- **What we explicitly do NOT rely on:** Financial engineering, leverage, or multiple expansion as the primary thesis. If the return doesn't work at flat multiples, we don't do the deal.

## Time Horizon

- **Typical hold period:** 4–6 years
- **Patience threshold:** 18 months before formal thesis reassessment. We expect J-curve investment in GTM during year 1.
- **View on timing vs. time in market:** We'd rather be 6 months early at the right price than try to time the perfect entry. Compounding rewards patience.

## Approach to Value Creation

- Deploy operating partners within 90 days of close for GTM strategy, pricing optimization, and org design
- Help hire VP Sales, VP Marketing, and CFO — the three roles that most often bottleneck scaling from $20M to $80M ARR
- Open LP network for enterprise customer introductions, especially in education, healthcare, and government verticals

## Edge / Information Advantage

- Our data pipeline identifies PLG companies with accelerating usage metrics 6–12 months before they formally fundraise. ~40% of our Fund I deals were sourced this way (pre-market or lightly marketed).
- Operating partners have direct pattern recognition on the $20M→$100M ARR scaling journey, having done it 3x each. This helps both in diligence (spotting structural problems) and post-investment (knowing what to fix first).

## What You Avoid

- "Founder-as-sole-genius" companies with no second layer of management. Key person risk kills returns.
- Companies where >40% of revenue comes from a single customer. Concentration risk is unhedgeable.
- Markets with clear winner-take-all dynamics where we'd be backing the #3 or #4 player. We invest in markets that support 2–3 large winners.
- Businesses that require heavy professional services to deliver the product. Services revenue at >20% of total is a pass.

## Intellectual Influences

- Bessemer's cloud investing framework — efficiency metrics, burn multiples
- Hamilton Helmer's 7 Powers — especially counter-positioning and switching costs
- Frank Slootman's operating philosophy — focus, intensity, execution speed
