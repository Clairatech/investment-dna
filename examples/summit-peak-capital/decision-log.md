# Decision Log

## Investments Made

### MedFlow Systems — March 2021

- **Decision:** Invest $18M at $150M post-money (Series B)
- **Thesis at time of decision:** Specialty clinic practice management is a whitespace. MedFlow's free scheduling tool is a PLG wedge into dermatology practices that expands to full practice management. 85% of target clinics use legacy on-prem systems or paper.
- **Key debate points in IC:** (1) Can a PLG motion work in healthcare where buying decisions are made by office managers, not clinicians? (2) Is dermatology-only a feature or a market limitation?
- **What tipped the decision:** Customer calls revealed that once scheduling was adopted, 70% of practices asked about billing integration within 6 months. Pull-through was organic.
- **Dissenting views:** Priya flagged concern about Epic expanding into small practices. Consensus was that Epic's cost structure makes sub-50-physician groups uneconomical for them.
- **Key risks acknowledged:** Single-specialty concentration; regulatory risk if telehealth reimbursement changes
- **Outcome (updated over time):** Top performer. $8M follow-on at Series C. Current valuation ~$520M. ARR at $42M with 145% NDR. Multi-specialty expansion underway. IPO candidate for 2026.

### DataForge Analytics — June 2021

- **Decision:** Invest $15M at $120M post-money (Series B)
- **Thesis at time of decision:** Mid-market analytics gap between enterprise BI (Tableau/Looker) and SMB tools (Google Analytics). DataForge's embedded approach lets non-technical teams build dashboards without SQL.
- **Key debate points in IC:** (1) Is "mid-market analytics" a real category or a wedge that gets squeezed from both sides? (2) Founder is technical and brilliant but has never managed a sales team — can he build GTM?
- **What tipped the decision:** Product NPS was 72 (exceptional for B2B analytics). Usage data showed daily active usage, not just monthly check-ins. Believed the product would sell itself.
- **Dissenting views:** Marcus argued that the founder needed a seasoned CRO before the round, not after. He was right.
- **Key risks acknowledged:** GTM execution risk; dbt + Looker ecosystem growing fast
- **Outcome (updated over time):** Growth slower than plan — ARR at $15M vs. $22M target. Product is strong but sales motion took 18 months longer than expected to build. New VP Sales hired Jan 2025. Thesis partially intact — watching closely.

### SupplyLens — April 2022

- **Decision:** Invest $20M at $160M post-money (Series B)
- **Thesis at time of decision:** Post-COVID supply chain visibility is a permanent priority for mid-market manufacturers. SupplyLens's procurement analytics platform replaces manual spreadsheet-based supplier management.
- **Key debate points in IC:** (1) Is the post-COVID urgency sustainable or will it fade? (2) 9-month average sales cycle for mid-market manufacturing seems long — can it be compressed?
- **What tipped the decision:** Three anchor customers (each >$500K ACV) validated the pain point. Pipeline was 4x committed revenue. Believed the macro tailwind would accelerate adoption.
- **Dissenting views:** James's analysis showed that comparable procurement software companies historically grew slower than our model assumed. His concerns were noted but overridden by the pipeline data.
- **Key risks acknowledged:** Long sales cycles; customer concentration in early stage; manufacturing sector cyclicality
- **Outcome (updated over time):** Below plan. ARR at $16M vs. $22M target. Supply chain urgency did fade from peak COVID levels. Sales cycle hasn't compressed. Product pivot to workflow automation is promising but unproven. On watch list.

### CodeLattice — January 2025

- **Decision:** Invest $25M at $280M post-money (Series B)
- **Thesis at time of decision:** AI-assisted code review becomes mandatory as AI-generated code increases. CodeLattice integrates into CI/CD pipelines (not IDE) which makes it sticky and measurable. 4,000+ teams on free tier, 12% paid conversion.
- **Key debate points in IC:** (1) GitHub Copilot bundling risk — will Microsoft build this into GitHub Actions? (2) $280M is a rich entry — can they grow into it?
- **What tipped the decision:** The PLG metrics were the strongest we'd ever seen — 4,000 teams with zero sales touch. Enterprise conversion happening bottoms-up without a sales team yet. Reminiscent of early Datadog adoption patterns.
- **Dissenting views:** Priya concerned about entry valuation (15x ARR). Sarah countered that at 120% YoY growth, the multiple normalizes quickly if they execute.
- **Key risks acknowledged:** Platform risk (GitHub); AI model commoditization; rich entry valuation
- **Outcome (updated over time):** Early — 3 months in. All leading indicators on or above plan.

## Notable Passes

### Vertebra Health — September 2023

- **Decision:** Pass
- **Why it was brought to IC:** AI-powered radiology diagnostics. Strong clinical validation, FDA clearance, growing revenue. Fit our healthcare thesis.
- **Reason for pass:** Revenue model was per-scan (transactional, not SaaS). Hospital procurement cycles were 18+ months. No PLG motion. Revenue at $6M but required heavy clinical sales team to grow.
- **What would have changed the decision:** If they had a SaaS pricing model with annual contracts and NDR >110%, we would have invested.
- **Outcome:** Raised Series B from a healthcare-specialist fund at $100M. Growing but burning cash heavily on sales. Our pass logic appears sound so far.

### QuickStack — February 2024

- **Decision:** Pass
- **Why it was brought to IC:** Developer tools for serverless deployment. Strong open-source community (15K GitHub stars). Revenue just hitting $8M ARR.
- **Reason for pass:** Founder was sole technical genius — no VP Eng, no second technical leader. When we asked about succession planning, the founder was dismissive. Key person risk was too high.
- **What would have changed the decision:** A strong technical co-founder or VP Eng hire.
- **Outcome:** They raised at $180M valuation from a tier-1 VC. Growing well. Key person risk hasn't materialized yet, but our process was right — we don't invest in that risk profile.

### PayBridge — November 2024

- **Decision:** Pass
- **Why it was brought to IC:** B2B payments infrastructure. $22M ARR, 60% growth. Fit the fintech infrastructure thesis.
- **Reason for pass:** 45% of revenue came from one enterprise customer (a large neobank). Customer concentration was an automatic pass per our criteria.
- **What would have changed the decision:** Customer concentration below 25%.
- **Outcome:** Still monitoring. The neobank customer is growing, which is good for PayBridge short-term but doesn't solve the concentration risk.

## Patterns & Lessons

### What We Get Right

- PLG signal detection — our best investments (MedFlow, CodeLattice) were sourced through PLG metrics before the market noticed
- Healthcare vertical SaaS conviction — we built real expertise and it's compounding. Our healthcare operating partner network gives us an edge in diligence.
- Customer call discipline — 8+ calls minimum has saved us from at least 3 bad investments where the product looked good but usage was shallow

### Common Mistakes

- Overweighting pipeline data in manufacturing/enterprise — SupplyLens taught us that pipeline in long-cycle industries converts at much lower rates than SaaS norms
- Underweighting GTM execution risk — DataForge had exceptional product metrics but we underestimated how hard it is to build a mid-market sales motion from scratch
- Anchoring on macro narratives — "supply chain is permanently changed" was partly right but the urgency faded faster than we modeled

### Decision Quality vs. Outcome

- **Good decision, bad outcome (so far):** SupplyLens — the process was sound, the thesis was defensible, but the sales cycle and macro assumptions didn't hold. We'd make the same decision with the same information.
- **Good decision, great outcome:** MedFlow — thesis, process, and execution all aligned. The PLG-in-healthcare insight was non-consensus and right.
- **Process gap we caught:** DataForge — Marcus's dissent on GTM readiness was correct and we should have weighted it more. Updated our IC process to require a formal GTM readiness assessment for any company without a VP Sales at time of investment.
