# Investment Criteria

## Must-Haves (Deal-Breakers if Missing)

- ARR ≥ $10M with ≥ 50% YoY growth (or ≥ $20M ARR with ≥ 30% growth)
- Gross margins ≥ 70%
- Net dollar retention ≥ 120%
- At least 2 C-suite executives with prior experience at a company that scaled past $50M ARR
- Clear product-market fit evidenced by organic customer acquisition (not just outbound-driven)
- North American HQ with primary revenue base in US/Canada

## Strong Preferences (Flexible but Important)

- Product-led growth motion (self-serve or freemium) contributing ≥ 30% of new logo acquisition
- CAC payback < 18 months
- Multiple product lines or clear expansion roadmap beyond initial wedge
- Existing enterprise customers (not purely SMB)
- Founded ≥ 3 years ago (post-early-stage chaos)

## Financial Thresholds

- **Minimum revenue:** $10M ARR
- **Revenue growth rate:** ≥ 50% YoY (or ≥ 30% if ARR > $20M)
- **Gross margin floor:** 70%
- **Unit economics requirement:** LTV/CAC ≥ 3x, CAC payback < 18 months
- **Valuation ceiling:** 30x ARR for >80% growth; 20x ARR for 50-80% growth; 15x ARR for 30-50% growth
- **Path to profitability:** Must show path to FCF positive within 24 months of our investment at current burn rate

## Team / Management Criteria

- **Founder profile:** Technical founder preferred; must demonstrate intellectual honesty about weaknesses and willingness to hire above themselves
- **Management team requirements:** At minimum, dedicated VP Engineering and VP Product. If no VP Sales, must have a plan and pipeline for the hire.
- **Board/governance expectations:** Board seat for Summit Peak; quarterly board meetings with formal financial and KPI reporting
- **Red flags:** Founder who has fired >2 senior hires in the last 12 months; co-founder conflict; CEO who won't delegate

## Market / Competitive Criteria

- **Minimum TAM:** $5B+
- **Market structure preference:** Fragmented incumbent market being disrupted by software (or vertical SaaS replacing horizontal tools)
- **Competitive moat requirements:** At least one of: proprietary data, deep workflow integration, network effects, regulatory compliance as a feature
- **Regulatory risk tolerance:** Moderate — we like regulated industries (healthcare, fintech, govtech) where compliance is a barrier to entry, but avoid companies whose core product legality is uncertain

## Deal Structure Preferences

- **Preferred instrument:** Preferred equity with standard protective provisions
- **Governance requirements:** Board seat, information rights, pro-rata follow-on rights, standard protective provisions (veto on M&A, new senior debt, change of control)
- **Syndication preference:** Lead or co-lead; minimum $10M check. Will not participate in rounds where no investor has governance.
- **Follow-on reserve strategy:** Reserve 40% of initial check for follow-on; max 2 follow-on rounds per company

## Automatic Passes

- Revenue <$10M ARR with no clear path to $10M within 12 months
- Companies where >40% of revenue is professional services
- Single-customer dependency >40% of revenue
- CEO unwilling to grant board seat or quarterly reporting
- Any business requiring ongoing fundraising to survive (< 12 months runway post-close)
- "Pivot stories" — companies that have fundamentally changed their product or market 2+ times
